Why It Matters

Monsoon-driven contamination events increase diarrhoeal outbreak risks. Early reports and sensors are crucial to reduce response times and save lives.

How It Works

  1. Water sensors stream hourly readings.
  2. ASHAs submit syndromic reports offline.
  3. Edge rules and cloud scoring generate alerts.
  4. Local SOPs guide investigation and intervention.

Our Technology in Action

Real-Time Sensing

Our network of low-cost sensors continuously monitors water quality indicators like turbidity and TDS, providing a constant stream of data from the source.

Intelligent Analysis

Data is analyzed in real-time by our machine learning models, which identify anomalies and calculate an 'outbreak score' to predict potential risks before they escalate.

Community Alerts

When a risk is detected, automated alerts are sent to community health workers and local authorities via an offline-first app, ensuring a rapid, coordinated response.
